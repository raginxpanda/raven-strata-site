The Problem

Paper forms don't protect your crews. They protect your filing cabinet.

Every utility runs pre-job briefings. Most run them on paper. And when something goes wrong, paper fails.

🔍
Can you produce last Tuesday's briefing in 60 seconds?
OSHA doesn't wait. After an incident, investigators want the briefing from that morning — the exact hazards discussed, who signed, what PPE was selected. Paper buried in a truck or a filing cabinet isn't compliance. It's liability.
⚡
Does your form know the difference between 4.16kV and 34.5kV?
Paper forms are static. A crew working 4.16kV checks the same boxes as a crew working 34.5kV transmission. The hazards are different. The PPE is different. The clearance distances are different. Paper doesn't adapt. Raven Strata does.
⚠️
When a near-miss happens at a location, who warns the next crew?
A cable strike on 5th and Main last month lives in a paper report in someone's desk. The next crew that goes to 5th and Main has no idea. Raven Strata remembers — and alerts every crew automatically.
☁️
Did this morning's briefing account for the weather at the job site?
Thunderstorm warnings, high wind, extreme heat — conditions that change by the hour. Paper forms can't check the forecast. Raven Strata pulls live weather the moment a crew arrives on site and adjusts the hazard checklist automatically.
The Solution

A briefing that thinks before your crew does.

Raven Strata is a mobile-first pre-job safety briefing platform designed exclusively for electric utility field crews.

Select a substation and the briefing builds itself
Feeder, voltage, hazards, PPE — all auto-populated from your infrastructure data. No typing. No guessing. No missed hazards.
Every crew member signs digitally
On-screen signatures with timestamps. You'll know who was briefed, when they signed, and what they acknowledged — not just a check mark on a clipboard.
Audit-ready PDF in one tap
Complete documentation with hazards, mitigations, crew attendance, GPS coordinates, weather conditions, and legal sign-off. Ready for OSHA before they ask.
Works offline — syncs when you're back
Full functionality without cell signal. Because crews don't wait for Wi-Fi at a broken pole in a thunderstorm.
